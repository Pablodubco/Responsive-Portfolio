About Me

So… there we were, just three average coworkers in a room. Cold sweat running down our backs, staring at a screen in utter despair. Almost half a year's worth of processed data defiled by a sadistic query that'd been run with the wrong parameters. Hundredths of man-hours lost in mere minutes, and we had to recover it by the end of the week. I was the “Excel guy” (every office's got one). There isn’t really much more to the story, no evil plot to conquer the mortgage credit business, no romance, no ultra hacking typing on two keyboards at the same time. We were done by the following day. A couple of things stuck with me.

First, coffee will kill me, and I’ll die a happy man. Secondly, that through careful planning, by relying on each other’s strengths, and just using a series of simple scripts, three persons were able to redo hundredths of hours of work in a single day.

I’ve since studied code on my own, learned to use SQL, S-plus, a little bit of java and C#, and, of course, honed even more my Excel-fu. I’m Pablo Dubcovsky, an economist that enjoys min-maxing everything. I love playing and breaking videogames (bugs are cool). I’m ashamed to admit it, but I've committed murder; my first Android phone didn’t survive me violating its partitions and OS, in the most despicable manner, just to see how it worked. This basic portfolio, as small and simple as it may seem, has proven to be a lot of fun, a good challenge, and an important step to acquire the skills needed to stay moving forward in this world, where the universal language that binds everything together is spoken by machines. Hope you enjoy it!
